5 Keys to Getting Smarter
July 16, 2016 Tweet

Scientific American recently published a study that explored if you can increase your intelligence. The good news: you can!

There are 5 activities you should do regularly to increase fluidity, which is how you gain capacity for learning and complex thought.

1.

"The joy of life comes from our encounters with new experiences, and hence there is no greater joy than to have an endlessly changing horizon, for each day to have a new and different sun.” ― Jon Krakauer, Into the Wild

The first key to getting smarter is to seek novelty. If you put your mind in new environments your brain will gain dexterity and resilience. For example, if you've been reading a lot, get physical. If you've been focussing on large things (cars, trees), turn to something delicate (needlepoint). If you've been indoors, go out. Do things you don't know if you'll be good at and you aren't even sure you'll like. Strive for an all-purpose brain by making friends with the unfamiliar.

2.

"One should not pursue goals that are easily achieved. One must develop an instinct for what one can just barely achieve through one's greatest efforts." — Albert Einstein

The second key to getting smarter is to challenge yourself. Undertake tasks that are difficult, like reading a dense book or solving a Sudoku puzzle. It's ok to feel discomfort; that's your brain strengthening. Just like at the gym, notice when you get too comfortable with the task. If it becomes easy, time to move on to a harder one.

3.

“I never made one of my discoveries through the process of rational thinking." ― Albert Einstein

The third key to getting smarter is to think creatively. When thinking about a problem, don't just rely on relevant facts. Pull in everything you know. Make connections between the logical, social, aesthetic and practical parts of you. Engaging all part of your brain creates neural pathways, and the more pathways, the greater the fluidity.

4.

“Don't wish it were easier. Wish you were better.” ― Jim Rohn

The fourth key to getting smarter is to do things the hard way. Efficiency is the enemy to brain training. Avoid it. Compose an email without the spell-checker. Drive somewhere with the GPS turned off. Translate a foreign-language website manually. Work. Be self-reliant.

5.

“First of all," he said, "if you can learn a simple trick, Scout, you'll get along a lot better with all kinds of folks. You never really understand a person until you consider things from his point of view--until you climb into his skin and walk around in it.” ― Harper Lee, To Kill a Mockingbird

The fifth and last key to getting smarter is to network. Expose yourself to new people. Throw yourself into situations in which to practice the skills above. You will learn new ideas from others and hear yourself articulate your own. With the emotional benefits that also come with being social, there's a lot to gain from getting out there!

So go ahead! Seek novelty, challenge yourself, think creatively, do things the hard way, and network. The result will be a stronger brain. You can do it.
