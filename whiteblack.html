The Recent History of Calling People 'White' and 'Black'
Sun Mar 6, 2016 Tweet

White people are not white. Nor are black people black. Even the palest individuals have tints of red, blue and pink. And the darkest tones don't approach black. And there isn't really a spectrum in between (what's blacker, Native American tones or Middle Eastern). The most recent politically correct phrases are "person of color" and "white".

Barring a tattoo sleeve, human skin falls into a pretty limited range of hues. So when and why did "color" become the identifying word for ethnicity?

For one, people of similar skin tones get treated similarily. If a racist person thinks you are black they will mistreat you, regardless of your actual heritage. But someone being mistaken for white or black happens as often as being mistaken for Middle Eastern versus Mediterranean.
