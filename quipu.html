Who Needs A Spreadsheet When You Have Dye And Some Rope?
Wed Jan 27, 2016
Tweet

The Incans of Peru had no known written language. Even so they kept excellent records on every details of their lives, from finances to geneologies. How could they create lasting records without written language? They used color and shape.

By twisting and knotting strands of dyed rope they told stories of how much cattle the owned, how their family tree was structured, and the shape of their personal finances.

These ropes-turned-data visualizations are called quipus. They may sound quaint, but have survived over 500 years: longer than our handwritten journals or digital spreadsheets ever will, and have kept us informed on the details of daily Incan life.
