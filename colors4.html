Part 4: Print Palette with 4 Colors

If you haven't yet, check out Part One and Part Two of this series which introduced color wheels and web-optimized palettes. Part three introduced print-optimized palettes.

In this tutorial we'll finish the series by making a print palette with 4 colors.

1. Pick a Starting Color

Using this print-optimized color wheel, choose any color you like as your base color. Once again I'm choosing red-orange, second from the top red.

2. Apply a Color Scheme

To get the rest of you colors apply a color scheme. For palettes with four colors you have three schemes to choose from: tetradic, square and analogous.

Tetradic

The tetradic scheme is vibrant and balanced. It consists of a base color and and three colors that are 60 degrees, 180 degrees, and 240 degrees from the base color.
