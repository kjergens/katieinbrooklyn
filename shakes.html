Think Shakespeare's Bedroom Was Drab? Think Again.
Thu Feb 11, 2016 Tweet

Ok, so you might not have been thinking about Shakespeare's bedroom. But now that you are, chances are you are picturing a room shrouded in browns, blacks, and grays. Because it was the 16th century and people in the past could only get their hands on neutral fabrics, right?

That's what the curators of Shakespeare's birthplace in Stratford-upon-Avon thought when they decorated the tourist destination with white walls, gray linens, and bare wooden bedposts.

But then in 2000, it was established that most middle-class families of the day, which would include Shakespeare's, decorated their homes with painted cloths and vibrant linens. Colorful textiles were a sign a wealth and anyone who could afford to showed it off.

His birthplace and adult home have been redecorated in the rich colors that surrounded him all his life.

Lucky man!
