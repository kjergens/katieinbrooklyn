For constitutions, one hundred years is old. Two hundred years is very old. Two hundred and twenty-seven years is a record. Which makes the U.S. constitution not just the longest-running in the world, it's a rare gem. It was amended a handful of times (most recently in 1992) but nothing was ever deleted, meaning the text written centuries ago is still legally valid.

This is not the case around the globe. 90% of all soveriegn nations adopted their constitutions after 1901. Of that, over 50% signed theirs after 1990! (Yes, the year Beverly Hills 90210 debuted.) Only the United States has one from the 1700s. Countries like Finland and France rewrote their constitutions to keep up with modern times. Countries like Spain and Italy became republics relatively recently.

Nations that have come and gone since the signing of the U.S. constitution: the Soviet Union, Yugoslavia, Czechoslovakia, Texas (a sovereign nation for 10 years), East Germany and Prussia.

The United Kingdom does not have a constitution.
