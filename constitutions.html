A visitor from another country could be forgiven for describing the U.S. Constitution as downright vintage. Acting as our base of laws for 227 years, the U.S. Constitution is by far the longest-running in the world.

Some numbers:

Over 50% of sovereign nations adopted their constitutions after 1990. A full 90% adopted theirs since the start of the 20th century. Only 1 (0.06%, the United States) wrote theirs in the 1700s.

It reminds us that nations like Italy and Spain became republics relatively recently. Countries like Finland have scrapped old versions and started anew.

Constitutions that have come and gone during the lifetime of the U.S.'s include those of the Soviet Union, Yugoslavia, Czechoslovakia, Texas (a sovereign nation for 10 years), East Germany and Prussia.
