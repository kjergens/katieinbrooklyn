The U.S. Constitution has been active for 227 years and as such it is the longest-running in the world, by a lot. You could rightfully call it vintage. It has been amended, most recently in 1992, but nothing is ever deleted (which is why in order to negate one amendment you need to write another). This structure means that all of the text written centuries ago is still valid.

This is not the case around the globe. Countries like Finland and France have rewritten their constitutions to keep up with modern times, while countries like Spain and Italy became republics relatively recently.

A full 90% of soveriegn nations adopted their constitutions after 1900 and over half after 1990. Only the United States has one from the 1700s.

Constitutions that have come and gone during the lifetime of America's: those of the Soviet Union, Yugoslavia, Czechoslovakia, Texas (a sovereign nation for 10 years), East Germany and Prussia.

The United Kingdom does not have a constitution.
