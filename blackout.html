The Importance of a Creative Blackout Hour
Wed Feb 24, 2016
Tweet

When you have an idea it's important to capture it, fast! Peel off to the side of the road; wake up, turn the lights on and find a pen; whip out the Notes app in the middle of a movie -- whatever it takes to trap it before it flies away.

But equally important is knowing when not to care. When you work on a creative project you are bombarded by your own ideas. "If I change a font here all the fonts have to change." "Should I never have used this font?" "Can I make a font?" "Am I taking time away from illustrating?" "What about the project I wanted to do before this one?" "I need to redo the navigation." "I need to redo all navigations from all my projects."

It gets oppressive.

At this point your brain is an idea-generating monster. The only way to stop it is to ignore it. Don't write down the new ideas. Don't let them breed.

This is a difficult. You risk losing a good idea. Many of us have had our best ideas after a trail of hopeless ones.

But if you never calm that monster your brain will not have room for fresh ideas. Even the best soil needs time to lie flat, unattended. Your creative mind deserves no less.
