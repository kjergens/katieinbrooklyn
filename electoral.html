Dec 17, 2016

In a presidential election, a New Yorker's vote has only 28% the weight of a Wyomingite's. Numbers like this have people questioning the relevance of the electoral college, which unevenly distributes votes across the states.

It's not just the coasts that get the short end of the electoral stick. While a Wyomingite owns 1/150,000 of the final vote (six-in-a-million), a poor Illinoisan controls just 1/500,000 (two-in-a-million) of the outcome.

To make your vote worth the most move to Wyoming, Vermont, or North Dakota. To make it worth the least move to New York, California or Florida.
